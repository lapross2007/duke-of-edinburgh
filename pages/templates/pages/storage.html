Storage

SSD – Solid State Drive - the newest form of storage these drives were very expensive at the time of release then they are now so like the price of HHD’s they really are only for mass storage. They come in different form factors 2.5” and smaller faster newer NVME drives that slot into the motherboard

M.2 NVME drives - these forms of SSD’s are cheaper than 2.5” SSDs in lower capacity like 512gb but more expensive in large capacity's like 2TB. They are the current fastest SSDs on the market right now.

HDD - Hard Disk Drive – this is a cheaper slower older form of storage. It is rarely used as a main drive nowadays, but many use it to store large games or applications. They are bought nowadays in TB (Terabytes) as they are slow anyway there is no point in getting a small capacity HDD. It stores info on a disk that spins to get the information off it, this means that it can take a while to access the information so that’s why they are not used as a main drive as it slows down the entire PC
