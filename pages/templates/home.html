PCs For Dummies

The list below are the main components, each part plays an important role in having a PC turn on and function for different tasks. For each part there is a brief summary on what the part does and a link to a more in depth description on the part.

The first part on the list is the CPU - Central Proccessing Unit - this piece is often known as the "brain" of the PC as it controls the other parts. To find out more click here.

Another vital piece of a PC is the GPU - Graphics Proccessing Unit - this part is what creates the images for display on a monitor. To get more information click here.

The motherboard is the component that links all the pieces together and allows the parts to communicate and work together to complete the task. For greater information click here.

The part for long term storage for information is made up of 3 main devices - NVME drives, Hard drives and SSDs - each work differently and the more in depth information on the pieces is here.

The short term, quick access storage is known as RAM - Random Access Memory - or memory. RAM is used for information that needs to be accessed quickly. For extra information you can click here.
